Press Release
For the first time, you can attend your OKR review without attending your OKR review.
Today at Google I/O, we are unveiling the flibblebit — a first-of-its-kind ambient intelligence device that acts, speaks, and defends your key results on your behalf while simultaneously transporting your consciousness somewhere you'd actually like to be.
OKR reviews are the single most common source of workplace dread. They demand preparation, presence, and the ability to confidently explain why your baseline moved by 0.3%. The flibblebit eliminates all three requirements.
The flibblebit ships with three core modes:
* Automate me — Deploys a large language model trained on thousands of OKR review transcripts to ensure every metric has a compelling narrative, a credible baseline, and a roadmap for improvement. It speaks fluent OKR, satisfies the metrics police, defends baselines, and you get the credit.
* Relax me — Uses haptic entrainment and binaural audio to guide the user into a light dream state. Surfing, snowboarding, or a custom scene of your choice. Your consciousness goes somewhere better while your metrics get managed.
* Alert me — Monitors conversation sentiment in real time and wakes you immediately if anything genuinely serious arises. Watches conversation heat like a hawk. Pulls you back only when it actually matters.

Customer FAQ
Q: What does "Automate me" actually do in a live review?
A: The flibblebit joins the call, monitors all questions in real time, and responds verbally through a synthesized voice matched to your own. It references your OKR data, defends baselines, and proposes improvement plans — all without your involvement.

Q: Will my teammates know I'm not there?
A: No. The voice synthesis and response latency are tuned to be indistinguishable from normal human engagement. The flibblebit even adds occasional "hmm" sounds and brief pauses for realism.

Q: Can I customize my dream destination?
A: Yes. The companion app lets you configure any scene — surfing, snowboarding, hiking, a sun lounger, deep sleep. Scenes can be linked to specific meeting types for automatic selection.

Q: How does "Alert me" know when to wake me?
A: The flibblebit monitors sentiment signals, speaker urgency, your name being invoked, and escalating vocabulary. You set your own sensitivity threshold — from "only if there's a major incident" to "anything above mild disagreement."

Q: What happens when I'm pulled back into a meeting?
A: Wake-up takes under 3 seconds. The flibblebit provides a 15-second audio brief on what's happening before un-muting you, so you can speak immediately with full context.


Internal FAQ
Q: How does the AI know what to say about a user's specific OKRs?
A: The flibblebit ingests connected data sources at setup — task managers, docs, dashboards. It builds a live context model that's refreshed before each meeting and can be manually reviewed in the app.

Q: What are the key technical risks?
A: Voice synthesis uncanny valley in small-group calls; the AI confidently stating a metric it can't verify; dream state desynchronization during abrupt wakeup. All three are active engineering workstreams.

Q: What's the go-to-market motion?
A: Direct to individual contributors first, then team lead bundles. Enterprise sales follows once IT policy guidance on AI voice proxies is clearer. Initial channel is Google Workspace Marketplace.

Q: What's the north star metric?
A: OKR reviews completed without user presence, divided by total OKR reviews attended. Target: 80% proxy rate at 90-day retention. Secondary: dream satisfaction score (DSat), measured via post-session survey.

Q: How are we thinking about the ethics angle?
A: We are commissioning an external review. Current position: the flibblebit is a productivity tool, not a deception tool, in the same category as email assistants and meeting summarizers. Guidance forthcoming.
